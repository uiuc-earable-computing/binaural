Learning to Separate Voices by Spatial Regions

Publish as conference paper at "International Conference on Machine Learning" - ICML 2022

Zhongweiyang Xu1, Romit Roy Choudhury1

1University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

Intro

In this project, we propose a self-supervised framework to solve the classical speech separation problem under two certain scenarios. First, we assume we have two microphones on 'earable' devices worn at the ears. Second, we do the separation by spatial regions, which means we first devide the spatial space into regions and then output a separated sound for each region, as shown below. Our method does not assume known number of sources and leverage the spatial cues provided by the HRTF for spatial separation. The result shows superior results of region-based separation comparing with classical all-source separation mechaism, while our method does not assume number of sources and is self-supervised.

Since the binaural recordings are rendered by the head related transfer functions (HRTFs) and HRTFs are personal, we believe these region-based separation model should be trained in a personalized way so that the separation result is the best for each person. However, personalized HRTF from users are not available, thus we propose to let the model learn from user's real-world personal bianural recordings in a self-supervised manner, as these recordings encode the HRTF information. Our self-supervision involves two stages: the preprocessing stage and the learning stage.

As shown above, the first stage takes real-world binaural mixture as input and use a spatial clustering module (classical signal processing based) to separate and select 'relatively clean' spatial sources. These sources form a personal database which generates a region-based training dataset by random mixing. The personalized dataset is then used in the second stage to train the dual-channel TasNet [1] for region-based separation.

The spatial clustering module in stage one is based on the DUET [2] paper, which aims to cluster the time-frequency bins by interaural time and level differences, as shown below. However this idea is challenging to implement in the binaural case due to spatial aliasing and unknown number of sources. We further improve on this idea to solve these problems and the detailed algorithm could be checked in our paper.


Results

We first do comparison between self-supervision with supervision for region-based separation:

  • GENERAL - Single general model trained with the CIPIC [3] HRTF database
  • PERSONALIZED* - Supervised training assume known HRTF
  • SELF - Self-supervised training without known HRTF
  • SEMI - Same as SELF but with half clean source
  • FEW-SEMI - Same as SELF but only 1hr of training
The result shows our self-supervision closes the gap between general model and ideal personalized model.

We then do comparison between region based separation with classical all-source separation. We only consider the case when there is only one speaker is the first region and compare the SNR for that single speaker. All-source separation model is the same achitecture and is trained assuming known perosnal HRTF and known number of sources. Even though, our self-supervised models can achieve 25% of gain roughly.
